Hi, I’m Bill. I like to build, create, and share.

My Current Journey

I’m looking to build one or more small internet-based businesses. I’ll do so using the latest and greatest tools to understand the state of the art and generate cash-flow.

I’m going to document my learnings in attempting to do the above. This should be of interest to:

  1. PMs / founders that want to see what someone with nothing to lose is trying to execute on and how they might be able to apply that in their product domains and product areas.
  2. Software engineers that might want to start a business of their own someday and want to understand the product / business side that they might not get exposed to in their day jobs.
  3. Solo-entrepreneurs or other product builders that are looking to do similar things.

I also advise and angel invest in startups in the data, AI, and machine learning domains.

My Past Journeys

I was founding PM and product management lead at Anyscale. I helped us grow from ~10 to 115 during my tenure. Building the initial PM team, launching the first version of our product, among many other hats.

Prior to Anyscale, I was an early solutions architect (and subsequently early PM, transiting to PM after my first year). While at Databricks, I was product maanger for the first version of Delta Lake, wrote Spark: The Definitive Guide for O’Reilly Press; co-authored with Matei Zaharia. I helped us scale from 80 people when I joined, to over 1300 when I left.

Prior to Databricks, I created SparkTutorials.net as a way to teach others the basics of Apache Spark. I was also creator of Data Analysis in Python with Pandas for Udemy and co-creator of, and first instructor for, Python for Data Science as a part of UC Berkeley’s Masters of Data Science Program.
